Should MVP include user accounts?

This affects database schema, auth infrastructure, and what personalization is possible from day one.

A

Public-only MVP — no accounts

Everything is readable without signing in. No auth infrastructure needed. Fastest to build, easiest to maintain. Add accounts in Phase 2 when you know what personalization users actually want.

Trade-off: No saved topics, no personalized alerts, no newsletter opt-in tied to a profile.

B

Email newsletter signup only

No full accounts — just an email capture for the daily brief digest. Simple, low-friction, and immediately useful for building an audience. No auth, no passwords, just a mailing list.

Trade-off: No personalization beyond what you send to everyone.

C

Lightweight accounts from day one

Email + password auth (or magic link). Enables saved topics, compliance alert preferences, and personalized dashboard in MVP. More upfront work but avoids a painful migration later.

Trade-off: Adds 1–2 weeks of dev time, requires email infra, and users have to register before getting value.
